Decision 2 · Why flagged

What actually drives the risk?

Leakage-safe Random-Forest importances. The signal is behavioural, not demographic — no protected attribute reaches the top tier. Switch between SHAP and permutation views.

Importance measure: 
Figure 2. Cleveland dot plot of leakage-safe drivers of course passing, ranked by mean |SHAP|. Marker shape encodes feature family: ● behavioural, ■ MPI, ▲ demographic.
Whiskers show ±1 SD of permutation importance over 10 repeats (displayed for the permutation measure; SHAP reports the mean only). Dashed line marks zero importance; 12 of 20 modelled features shown. Removing leakage lowers AUC only from 0.983 to 0.969, confirming the model learns behaviour rather than restated grades.

Read it as

The strongest driver is the proportion of active course weeks — a pure engagement signal — followed by assessment and submission activity. The three Motivation Proxy Index dimensions enter as incremental, interpretable contributors. Removing leakage barely dents performance (AUC 0.983 → 0.969), confirming the model learns behaviour, not a restated grade.

Decision 3 · The motivational story

The Motivation Proxy Index profile

Standardised MPI dimensions by outcome. Distinction/Pass learners lean on engagement and timeliness; Fail/Withdrawn invert it. Toggle outcomes to compare profiles.

Show outcomes: 
Figure 3. Motivation Proxy Index profile by final outcome (standardised dimension means). Outcomes are distinguished by line style and marker, not colour.
Radial axis spans −0.32 to +0.42 SD. Distinction and Pass profiles expand on engagement (D1) and timeliness (D3); Fail and Withdrawn contract on them. Two dimensions (D2 diversity, D4 persistence) run against theoretical expectation (see validity panel).

Decision 4 · Equity

Is the alert fair — and can we fix the gap?

True-positive rate (recall on students who actually pass) by group. A group-aware decision threshold closes the equal-opportunity gap for repeat-attempt students at almost no accuracy cost. Toggle mitigation on/off.

Decision threshold: 
Figure 4. Connected-dot (dumbbell) plot of true-positive rate by prior-attempt group: ○ standard 0.50 threshold, ● group-aware threshold; the connector spans the change.
The between-group equal-opportunity (TPR) gap falls from 0.077 to 0.047 at a cost of −0.2 pp accuracy and unchanged AUC (0.969). Group sizes: prior attempts > 0, n = 1,051; prior attempts = 0, n = 7,098. Use the toggle to emphasise either threshold.
